The Problem

Mathematics has perfect memory for what worked, and almost none for what didn’t.

Formal libraries preserve every theorem that passed. But the failed formulations, the dead routes, the exact constraint each failure carved out of the search space — those live in notebooks, chat logs, and the heads of the people who tried. When they leave, the field re-walks the same dead ends. Machines inherit the same amnesia at a million times the speed.

  • KEPT Theorems, proofs, definitions — the positive library
  • LOST Failed formulations and why each one died
  • LOST The constraint each failure proved about the search space
  • LOST Which routes survive and what evidence would close them
  • LOST Years of attempt history when a campaign changes hands
The System

A live map of proof pressure — and the machinery to act on it.

OSMM turns a formal library and a research history into persistent, typed state: what is proved, what has failed, which constraints those failures created, which routes still survive, and exactly what evidence would close the remaining gap.

proved routes banked walls open residuals the goal
01 · NEGATIVE KNOWLEDGE

Every failure becomes an asset.

A failed attack is banked as a typed wall: what it killed, the constraint it extracted, and which route inherits that constraint. Failure becomes a permanently smaller search space. This is the negative twin of a theorem library.

02 · THE RESIDUAL

Know exactly what remains.

Compose the positives, walls, and transports, and the system computes the residual cut-set: the minimal set of open objects separating you from closure. How boxed-in is this proof? Now that question has an answer.

03 · GRAMMAR & TRANSPORT

Move results between worlds — with receipts.

A typed grammar (the δ machinery) carries results among mathematical, physical, and semantic representations through checked transport squares. Theorems become typed tools that compose instead of prose that decays.

04 · BOUNDED AGENTS

Dispatch research, not chat.

Any residual can become a signed, finite research contract: one writer, a fixed deterministic gate, a typed terminal state. Agents attack the frontier under the Foreman. The map contracts only when the judge admits a receipt.

Four Levels Deep

From seeing the frontier to closing it.

LEVEL 1

SEE.

Load any registered goal and inspect its dependency cone, theorem-grade edges, killed formulations, and live survivors.

LEVEL 2

UNDERSTAND.

Know why the problem is boxed in, which assumptions carry each route, and which missing object would collapse the remaining distance.

LEVEL 3

ACT.

Apply typed theorem tools through checked transports, or turn a residual into a bounded contract and send agents against it.

LEVEL 4

CLOSE.

The system selects the binding residual, compiles a legal attack, runs it, and deposits the verified result back into the map.
